Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli born 5 November 1988 is an Indian international cricketer and former captain of the Indian national team who plays as a right-handed batsman for Royal Challengers Bangalore in the IPL and for Delhi in Indian domestic cricket. Widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen of all time, Kohli holds the record for scoring most runs in both T20 internationals and in IPL. In 2020, the International Cricket Council named him as player of decade. Kohli has won Man of the Tournament award twice at the ICC World Twenty20, in 2014 and 2016. Playing for his franchise in IPL, he won the Orange Cap and Most-valuable Player Award in 2016 season. Furthermore has won most player of the match and series awards in T20I. With 40 wins in 68 Test matches, he is India's most successful Test captain.

Kohli has numerous records to his name, including being the fastest player to score 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 11,000 runs in ODIs. He has captained India to many significant victories, including a historic Test series win in Australia. Off the field, Kohli is known for his fitness regime, philanthropic efforts, and business ventures, including a cricket academy and various brand endorsements. As a cricketer and a role model, Kohli continues to inspire millions with his passion and dedication to the sport.

Virat Kohli: A Journey Through the Years

  • 2002 – At just 13, Virat gets his first big break, joining Delhi’s Under-15 team for the Polly Umrigar Trophy. He tops the charts as the highest scorer for his team. The journey begins!
  • 2006 – After impressing at the youth level for Delhi, Virat is called up to the India U-19 team. He tours England and completely dominates, averaging over 100 in the one-day series. The young star is on the rise.
  • 2008 – Leading from the front, Virat captains India to victory in the Under-19 World Cup in Malaysia. His leadership and consistency catch everyone’s attention.
  • 2008 (June) – RCB snatches up a teenage Virat on a youth contract for ₹12.5 lakhs. Who knew this was the beginning of something massive?
  • 2008 (August) – Virat debuts for the Indian ODI squad in an away series against Sri Lanka. In game 4, he scores his first half-century, showing the world what he’s made of.
  • 2009 – It’s been a bit of a wait, but Virat announces his arrival with a bang! His maiden ODI century (107 off 111 balls) comes against Sri Lanka. The future icon is now a rising star.
  • 2011 – A crucial member of India’s World Cup-winning squad, Virat plays every single match. His quote after India’s victory becomes legendary: "Sachin Tendulkar has carried the burden of the nation for 21 years. It's time we carry him on our shoulders."
  • 2012 – Virat smashes his first Test hundred in a high-pressure game against Australia in Adelaide. He survives a near run-out with Ishant Sharma at the crease, but his brilliance isn’t enough to prevent a series loss.
  • 2013 – Stand-in captain? No problem. Virat steps up when MS Dhoni is injured during India's West Indies tour. Earlier that year, he also takes over as captain of RCB, his IPL team.
  • 2014 – It’s Kohli’s first taste of Test captaincy, filling in once again for an injured MS Dhoni. And he celebrates in style, scoring centuries in both innings at Adelaide. Talk about leading by example!
  • 2015 – Virat ventures into the world of sports ownership. Already co-owning ISL football club FC Goa, he now invests in the IPTL tennis franchise, UAE Royals. A multi-sport enthusiast, anyone?
  • 2016 – One of the defining moments of his career comes at the ICC World T20, where he plays a jaw-dropping knock of 82* off 51 balls, guiding India to the semi-finals. That innings against Australia in Mohali? Pure class!
  • 2017 – Kohli breaks numerous records, including the fastest player to score 8,000, 9,000, and 10,000 runs in ODIs. His consistency solidifies his place among cricket's all-time greats.
  • 2018 – Virat leads India in a historic Test series win against Australia in Australia. He showcases his batting prowess, scoring multiple centuries in the series and further establishing his captaincy credentials.
  • 2019 – Kohli leads India to the semi-finals of the ICC Cricket World Cup. He finishes as the team's top scorer, proving once again that he can rise to the occasion on the biggest stages.
  • 2020 – Despite the pandemic, Virat launches his fitness app and continues to focus on fitness and mental well-being. He also becomes a father, welcoming his first child, marking a new chapter in his life.
  • 2021 – Kohli steps down as T20I captain after the World Cup. He leads a young Indian side in the Test series against New Zealand, continuing to inspire the next generation.
  • 2022 – Virat returns to form, scoring his 71st international century and breaking his long-standing drought. He also plays a crucial role in India's T20 World Cup campaign, showing resilience and determination.
  • 2023 – As a senior member of the squad, Kohli plays a key role in India's successful Asia Cup campaign, solidifying his legacy as one of the game's greatest. He is celebrated for his sportsmanship and dedication to the sport.
  • 2024 – Virat continues to be a vital player for India, leading a new generation of cricketers while also expanding his business ventures. He launches a cricket academy aimed at nurturing young talent and promoting fitness, inspiring the next wave of athletes. His influence on and off the field solidifies his status as a cricketing legend.
